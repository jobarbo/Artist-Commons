### Key Takeaway

- Seeing is never neutral. Every era builds an optical order.
- Linear perspective reorganized the world, placing the viewer at the center and turning space into something measurable, ownable, governable.
- From brunelleschi's peephole to Leonardo's maps, vision became a technology of control; a wau to manage land, bodies and resources
- Modernity inherited this gaze. Cities, landscapes and even people were redesigned to fit the view from above, the view of the instituions, markets and administrations
- Contemporary artists mimc powers viewpoint to reveal it's logic and fracture perspective to restor conflict, memory and multiplicity.

### Key takeaways

- Photography and film roke the monopoly of the human eye. Vision became mechanical, repeatable, mobile.
- Machines began to see what we could not: the too-fast, the too-small, the too-distant, the hidden. The camera re-edited perception, revealing an optical unconscious beneath ordinary sight.
- Once vision is mediated ,reality is no longer singular. It can be frozen, stretched, dissected, accumulated, or haunted.
